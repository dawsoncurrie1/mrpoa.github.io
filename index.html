The Moon River Property Owners' Association represents property owners from Bala Falls to Ragged Rapids — advocating for our waterway, building community, and preserving what makes this place extraordinary.

Who We Are

Three Pillars of the Moon River Community

MRPOA members cherish the Moon River and its watershed. We represent and inform members, organize community events, and work to protect the environmental health of our river.

🌊

Advocacy & Representation

We represent waterfront property owners on issues that matter — noise bylaws, short-term rental licensing, water level management, arena redevelopment, and municipal planning. The larger our membership, the louder our collective voice.

🌳

Environmental Stewardship

Annual water quality testing since 2007, the Moon River Code for responsible use, shoreline buffer education, dark sky friendly lighting advocacy, and partnerships with the Muskoka Lakes Association to protect our watershed.

🎭

Community & Social Events

From the Jazz Cruise to the Family Fun Day Regatta, the Fishing Derby, and the Golf Tournament — MRPOA brings Moon River families together. Our spring and fall meetings keep the community connected year-round.

Community Standards

The Moon River Code

A shared commitment to responsible enjoyment of our river and lands. We ask all property owners and renters to observe these principles.

⛵ Safe Boating

Know the river before you go. Respect other boaters, watch for swimmers, reduce wake near shorelines, and don't circle repeatedly in one area.

💧 Water Quality

No washing or detergents in the river. Use septic-friendly products. Prevent fuel spills. Maintain natural shoreline vegetation to filter storm water.

🌙 Dark Sky Friendly

Shield outdoor lighting to shine downward only. Keep light from crossing onto neighbouring properties. Preserve our stunning Muskoka night sky.

🎧 Sound & Fireworks

Amplified sound should not be heard beyond your property. Limit fireworks to Victoria Day, Canada Day, Simcoe Day, and Labour Day.

🦉 Wildlife Safety

Keep garbage in bear-proof containers. Clean and cover BBQs. Keep pet food indoors. Don't feed waterfowl.

🏢 Renters Welcome

If you rent your property, please ensure your guests are aware of and observe the Moon River Code. Share a copy with every booking.

Environmental Values

Stewardship Principles

The environment is the economy in Muskoka. Our principles align with the Official Plans of the Township and District to ensure a sustainable natural environment for generations.

Natural Form First

Natural form should predominate over built form. From the water, the less visible buildings and structures are, the better for everyone.

Shoreline Buffers

Maintain a natural plant buffer along your waterfront, interrupted only by paths and docks on less than 25% of shoreline. This filters runoff and screens views.

Minimize Hard Surfaces

Use gravel instead of asphalt where possible. Let rain and snow seep naturally into the ground to recharge the water table and filter contaminants.

Dark Sky Lighting

Shield all outdoor lighting at the top to shine downward. Illuminate only what's below — not the sky, not your neighbour's property.
