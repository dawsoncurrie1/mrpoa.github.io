What's happening

The Township of Muskoka Lakes is about to commit the $192,400 Swift River reserve fund to a single Bala Town Dock project on Lake Muskoka at the June 10 Council meeting.

This contradicts the August 2025 community consultation, which clearly showed residents wanted several smaller projects, not one large legacy project.

Two of the survey's top three priorities have already been funded elsewhere — crosswalks on Muskoka Road 169 (District of Muskoka, June 2026) and the Moon River Boat Launch (2027 Capital Budget). The #1 result was inflated by people whose other top picks are no longer on the table.

Council's own September 2025 resolution directed staff to develop a list of capital improvements (PLURAL) aligned with survey priorities. The May 2026 recommendation funds only one project, contradicting that direction.

The fund exists because Swift River leased Township lands during the dam construction in Bala. Moon River residents bore the heaviest impact — yet the recommended project is on Lake Muskoka. Moon River receives nothing. Bala parks, sidewalks, and beautification receive nothing.

These funds have been held in reserve since 2020. There is no rush to commit them this month if the basis for the recommendation no longer reflects available options.
